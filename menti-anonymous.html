Background

Historically, participants in Mentimeter were always anonymous by default. In order to prevent harassment and offer better data tracking over time, however, we needed to introduce a way for users to add an identity to their actions in a presentation.

While the long term plan was to let users create their own accounts for participation, the immediate goal was to offer a simple way in platform for users to create a name and tie it to their actions in a presentation.

Our approach

To help users quickly include participant data in their presentations, we focused on the following:

  • Establishing a naming convention surrounding anonymity and identity in the platform
  • Build new entry points for a naming flow during participation, as well as naming signifiers throughout the user journey
  • Building and testing clickable prototypes in UX Research
  • Creating new settings for user accounts to accommodate more complex tools and features
  • Redefining the language we use at a system level when referring to privacy, anonymity, and identity throughout the platform
